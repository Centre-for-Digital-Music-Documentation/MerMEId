Entering special characters

The following is a list of selected unicode characters that you may copy and paste into your data. Their entity equivalents (e.g. '&#266d;') are shown for reference. Be aware, however, that the correct displaying of these characters may depend on the fonts available on the user's computer and/or the way your data are processed for display.

Music symbols

These three accidentals are supported by a number of common fonts and are fairly safe to use. MerMEId's HTML preview furthermore supports them by providing an embedded font (see below).

Additional music symbols

  • The following music symbols are very rarely supported by Unicode fonts yet and should be used with caution. MerMEId embeds the Euterpe font to display them correctly in the HTML preview.
    If files are used in other contexts, be aware that these characters may need special treatment in order to be displayed as expected. If so, you may want to use the free Unicode music font Euterpe by Ben Laenen. The font is available for download here.
